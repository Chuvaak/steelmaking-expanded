Boilers burn coal under a water vessel and push steam into a pipe network. Boiling 1 L of water yields 16 L of steam, at 160–220 °C depending on how full the vessel is (low water boils hotter).

Construction. Place the boiler block, then rightmouse it repeatedly with the required materials in your hotbar to raise it through its construction stages. The boiler also needs a masonry firebox built around its base — ctrl + shift + rightmouse shows the outline, and the chat reports what is missing.

Cornish Boiler (the starter) — stages consume:
- 42 x Metal Plate (24 must be iron)
- 26 x Metal Nails & Strips
- 14 x Metal Rod
- 48 x Fire Brick
Firebox masonry: 33 x Fire Brick Blocks, 1 x Coke Oven Door, 2 x Fire Brick Pipe Passthrough, 1 x Fire Brick Passthrough Bend (up), 1 x Fire Brick Pipe Outlet (up), one empty slot for the coal pile.
Output: up to 32 L/s of steam, chokes at 5 atm. Vessel holds 800 L; needs 150–500 L of water.

Lancashire Boiler (the upgrade) — stages consume:
- 102 x Steel Plate
- 66 x Metal Nails & Strips
- 12 x Metal Rod
- 64 x Fire Brick
Firebox masonry: 39 x Fire Brick Blocks plus the same door, passthroughs and outlet as the Cornish.
Output: up to 48 L/s of steam, chokes at 12 atm. Vessel holds 1200 L; needs 200–800 L of water.

Operating a boiler
1. Hold rightmouse on the lid to open it, pour in water (buckets, or pipe pressurised water into the intake from a fluid pump — it auto-fills to a safe level), and close the lid.
2. Lay a coal pile in the firebox slot behind the door and ignite it.
3. After about three minutes of heating the boiler starts boiling and pushes steam out its top outlet — pipe it to your engines.
4. The firebox exhaust must breathe: vent its outlet through a chimney or pipe. If exhaust back-pressure rises above ~0.8 atm the fire goes out.

Dangers. A boiler that keeps firing with nowhere to push steam sits at its choke pressure for ~30 seconds, then explodes. An open lid vents steam harmlessly (≈200 L/s) — your emergency release. An unpiped steam outlet just leaks. Feeding pressurised water into a boiling vessel flashes extra steam and spikes the pressure — throttle pumped feed water with a valve. The Cornish boiler's 5 atm ceiling is above the Watt engine's 4 atm breaking point: put a pressure valve between boiler and engine and you have a self-regulating line.
