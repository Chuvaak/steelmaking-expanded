Have you ever wanted to watch machines work and steam boil? This mod adds steam power to Vintage Story, along with all the piping the steam engines and boilers need to run.

The pipe network was originally built to move gases around the Blast Furnace from my other mod Steelmaking Expanded. Since the piping and steam power stand well on their own, I split them into this mod and reworked them to be more immersive.

Compatibility

  • Game version: built for Vintage Story 1.22. Legacy 1.20 and 1.21 builds are on the GitHub releases page.
  • Multiplayer: works in singleplayer and on dedicated servers.
  • Requires: Expanded Library (exlib).
  • Languages: English, Russian, Ukrainian.
  • Save safety: safe to add to an existing world. Removing it leaves the placed machine and pipe blocks behind, so break them first if you uninstall.

Quick start

The smallest self-sufficient steam line, start to finish:

  1. Place a Fluid Intake on a pond, over a three-deep column of water.
  2. Build a Cornish Boiler with its firebox, fill it through the top hatch with buckets, and light a coal pile behind its door. Vent the firebox through a chimney.
  3. Raise a Watt Engine nearby and pipe the boiler's steam outlet to its inlet through a Pressure Valve gated at about 2-3 atm.
  4. Drop a sub-machine into the engine's drive cell: a Fluid Pump (pipe the intake into it and its output back into the boiler to close the water loop), or a Mechanical Power Generator (run its axles to your machines).
  5. Route the engine's condensate drain back toward the boiler. That is a steam plant that feeds itself.

The in-game handbook has full articles under "Steam Power:" if you want the detail.

Commands and config

  • .exmod network hi / unhi - highlights every networked block, a separate colour per network, so you can see where a run is continuous and where it is broken. Updates live as you build.
  • .exmod measure metric / imperial - switches display units on pipes and machines (L, atm, degrees Celsius or gal, psi, Fahrenheit). Display only; the simulation is unchanged.
  • /exmod config ppex - view or change any gameplay value live, applied immediately with no world reload (admin).
  • /exmod recipes ppex <normal|cheap> - switch the crafting and construction cost level; applies on the next world reload (admin).

Two config files are generated under VintagestoryData/ModConfig/ on first run:

  • ppex_values.json - the gameplay tunables (pipe capacity and burst pressures, boiler steam output and water draw, engine steam consumption). Editable live with /exmod config ppex.
  • ppex_recipes.json - the per-recipe and per-construction cost numbers for each level (normal and cheap). Pick the active level with /exmod recipes ppex and fine-tune the numbers in this file; changes apply on the next world reload.

How it works

Pipe network (click to expand)

This mod adds iron and steel pipes with straight, bend and junction nodes. They connect automatically into a shared network that carries gases or fluids between structures and machines. The network simulates:

  • Medium - each network holds one type of material at a time: air, exhaust, steam or water.
  • Temperature - whatever pushes material into the pipes can heat it, and that heat travels to the machines downstream.
  • Pressure - measured in atm (gauge). Each pipe contributes 30 L of capacity to the shared pool.
    • Gases compress, so they can exceed a pipe's nominal volume and raise the pressure (pressure = current volume / capacity). A single pipe of 30 L holding 60 L of gas reads 2 atm. Gases never exceed the pressure of whatever pushes them in, so steam from a 5 atm boiler tops out at 5 atm.
    • Liquids do not move on their own - a powered fluid pump drives them, or a running engine pushes its condensate in. They do not compress, so they transfer the driving pressure directly: a pump on an engine fed 3 atm steam outputs water at about 2.25 atm (engine efficiency is 0.75).
  • Leaks - an uncapped connector face leaks. A leaking run cannot hold more than 1 atm and bleeds about 8 L/s of gas or 10 L/s of liquid. Cap every end against a block, valve, machine port or outlet.
  • Bursts - an over-pressured pipe eventually bursts, splitting the network and spawning leaks. Iron bursts above 5 atm, steel above 10 atm; the weakest pipe sets the limit for the whole run.

A few special blocks let you shape the network:

  • Valve - severs the network when closed, a normal node when open. Separates flows and gates a machine's feed.
  • Pressure valve - a directional bridge that overflows from one network to another once the input side rises above its set gate. Holds a network at the pressure a machine needs.
  • Steam condenser - the only way to bridge a gas and a fluid: it injects steam into a running water flow, condensing it into the water network.
  • Outlets, passthroughs and fluid intakes - cross a structure wall, connect to a machine face, or draw fresh water from a pond.
Boilers and engines (click to expand)

A boiler fills with water and heats it until it boils into steam. Both boilers are multiblock structures containing one large vessel block, so you finish both the multiblock and the vessel to make them operational.

  • Cornish boiler - a medium vessel for low-pressure steam: 2 L/s of water into 32 L/s of steam, up to 5 atm. Cheaper, can be built entirely from iron - a good starting point.
  • Lancashire boiler - a larger vessel: 3 L/s of water into 48 L/s of steam, up to 12 atm. The pressure you need for the more advanced engines.

Fill a boiler through the top hatch with buckets (you can also draw water back out by hand). Once running, a fluid-pump engine keeps it topped up, so a hand-filled boiler runs until its coal burns out. The hatch doubles as an emergency vent. An over-pressured boiler can explode, so watch it.

A steam engine is a large block with a sub-machine slot. In the cell under its drive rod (opposite the cylinder) you place a sub-machine that turns the engine's power into work.

  • Watt engine - runs on low-pressure steam. Operating band 2-4 atm; within it the engine draws 30 L/s of steam.
  • Cornish engine - higher pressure and throttleable. Normal: 6-8 atm, 16 L/s. Throttled down: 5 atm, 8 L/s. Throttled up: 7 atm, 32 L/s. Throttling halves or doubles the engine's power.

Both engines break if over-pressured. One Cornish boiler supplies one Watt engine, which keeps the early math simple; moving up to a Lancashire boiler and Cornish engine gives far more power and more to balance.

The sub-machines - two here, one more from smex:

  • Fluid pump - pushes fluid from its bottom connection out the front. Needs a fluid intake in the bottom network. A Watt engine yields about 5 L/s of water.
  • Mechanical Power Generator - turns the engine's power into MP through axle connections; a Watt engine comfortably drives a couple of helve hammers.
  • Air blower (from smex) - pressurises air to the levels the blast furnace and Bessemer converter need; its output pressure is capped by the engine's inlet steam pressure.

Placing a sub-machine in the correct cell links it to the engine automatically. Every engine has a built-in steam condenser, so it recycles some steam; connect a pipe to the right side of the engine to route that condensate back to your boilers.

FAQ

  • My pipe will not go above 1 atm. A connector face somewhere is open and leaking. Cap every end against a block, valve, machine port or outlet.
  • My boiler exploded. It was over-pressured. Vent through the top hatch, or gate the steam line with a pressure valve.
  • My engine will not run, or it broke. The steam pressure is outside its band (Watt 2-4 atm, Cornish 6-8 atm), or it was over-pressured. Regulate the steam.
  • My sub-machine does nothing. It must sit in the cell under the engine's drive rod, opposite the cylinder. Placed there it links automatically.
  • I see unknown blocks after updating. Save migrations run on load; if any slipped through, replace them in creative.

Notes

  • The engines and sub-machines share a generic implementation, so adding a new one is mostly model and animation work - which I find tedious, so more may come later.
  • Because the steam system is standalone, a steam turbine and generator for electricity would be possible if a popular electrical-power mod ever makes it worthwhile.
  • Connecting everything takes a lot of pipe, so be ready for that.
  • This is the second mod to spin out of my first one, so there may still be bugs. Please report them and I will try to fix them quickly. Balance suggestions are welcome in the comments.
