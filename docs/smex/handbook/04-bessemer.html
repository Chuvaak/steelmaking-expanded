The Bessemer Converter takes molten iron and blows it into steel. It is the most demanding machine in the mod — it draws both mechanical power and a Blast gas supply, and only works as the final stage of an already-running molten line. Do not attempt it until your iron tier is established.

Building it. The converter is a 3x3x3 vessel raised through right-click construction. Place its control block first, then rightmouse it with 4 rusty gears and 12 iron or steel rods in your hotbar to start the vessel, and build up the construction stages until it reports complete. Around the vessel sit its service ports: a gas intake, a mechanical transmission, an input tap, and an output.

Blocks required:
- 1 x Bessemer Control (the control block, placed first)
- 1 x Bessemer Converter vessel — raised in place from 4 rusty gears + 12 iron/steel rods
- 1 x Bessemer Transmission
- 1 x Bessemer Gas Intake
- 1 x Molten Canal (Start)
- 2 x Molten Canal (Straight)
- 1 x Molten Canal (Tap)

Construction cost — raising the vessel. All stages together consume:
- 40 x Iron/Steel Plate
- 42 x Iron/Steel Nails & Strips
- 18 x Iron/Steel Rod
- 84 x Refractory Brick (tier 3)
- 84 x Fire Clay
- 3 x Gas Piping (Straight)

Plugging it in. Two services from the systems you already built:
- Mechanical power — connect an axle to the transmission so the vessel can tilt and blow.
- Blast — pipe Blast (from an air blower) into the gas intake. The intake must face the same way as the control block or it won't couple.
Then run a molten canal from a furnace tap into the converter's input tap, and a second canal off its output toward your molds.

Operating it.
1. Set the converter to Filling and pour molten iron in from the canal until charged.
2. Switch to Normal. With power and blast flowing, it refines the charge at ~1800 °C for about five minutes into steel.
3. Set it to Pouring to drain the finished steel into the output canal — from there it travels the molten network exactly like iron.

The same temperature rule applies inside the vessel: if the charge cools and solidifies before you pour it, break the converter with a steel pickaxe to recover the metal, then rebuild.
