Steelmaking Expanded adds a full industrial pipeline that works in two stages. Stage I stands up a blast furnace and its support machinery to smelt iron in bulk. Stage II bolts a Bessemer converter onto that line to refine the molten iron into steel. Build the iron stage first and get it running reliably — the steel stage is expensive and feeds directly off it.
Most of these structures need tier 3 refractory bricks, which means you need to reach the steel age before building them!

The chain is described in four companion articles:
- The Blast Furnace — the heart of the operation and its hoppers.
- Hot Blast & Gas Handling — cowper stoves, the smoke stack and the gas network.
- Molten Metal & Casting — canals, taps, molds, barrels and slag.
- The Bessemer Converter — stage II, blowing iron into steel.
The pipes, boilers and steam engines that power it all come from the companion mod — see Steam Power.

A production run, in short
1. Stock the reinforced hopper with crushed iron ore, crushed coke and lime, and enable dropping on the bell hopper.
2. Let the shaft fill with blast mix, ignite it, and close the door.
3. Push Air through a charged cowper stove into the tuyeres as hot blast (an air blower on a steam engine provides the Blast); vent spare exhaust through the smoke stack.
4. Watch the temperature climb past ~1482 °C into the Melting phase; alternate your two cowper stoves so the blast never goes cold.
5. When iron and slag have pooled, open the molten metal taps to drain them down the canals into molds or barrels — before anything solidifies.
6. Keep the hopper supplied, the exhaust flowing, and repeat.

Once the iron line runs smoothly, wire the converter into it and the same canals will carry steel.
