Molten Canals. Liquid metal is plumbed, not carried. Lay a Molten Canal (Start) as the anchor of a network, then run straights, bends and junctions from it — they are crafted from cobblestone and fire clay, and each canal keeps the look of the rock you built it from. Drain the furnace with its Molten Metal Taps — the lower tap pours iron, the upper tap pours slag — each into the start of a canal below it; toggle a tap with rightmouse. A Canal Tap at the far end pours the metal back out.

Sealing — a manual valve. rightmouse an empty straight canal with 4 fire clay and it caps over, severing the network at that block. Only a drained canal can be sealed. Reopen it with a chisel (recovers 2 fire clay).

Mind the temperature. Metal in the canals slowly cools; if it drops too far it solidifies in place — a hardened canal stops flowing and must be chipped clear (chisel + hammer once fully cold; you recover most of the metal as bits, and those bits crush back into crushed iron for the furnace). Keep runs short, keep them fed, and empty them before they or the furnace go cold.

Casting. A canal tap pours into two destinations:
- A Mold Pedestal casts small tool molds: shift + rightmouse places or removes the mold, ctrl + rightmouse toggles pouring. This mod adds larger casting molds — plate, quad rod and double ingot — that you clay-form and fire like any tool mold. The big anvil and helve hammer molds go under the canal tap itself instead.
- A Molten Barrel — bulk storage for liquid metal. Use it to buffer excess iron, or to hold slag from the top tap.

Handling hot molds. A freshly filled mold is dangerously hot: carrying it bare-handed burns you (wear heavy leather or blacksmith's gloves). While the metal is still liquid, the mold can only ride in your empty active hand — picking it up with anything else in hand, stashing it in a bag, or racking it spills the metal instantly. Set it down and let it harden, then collect the casting with a plain rightmouse.

Slag is not waste. Solidified slag grinds into powdered slag: mixed with slaked lime in a barrel it yields mortar, and spread on farmland it acts as a phosphate fertilizer — furnace byproduct into building stock and crop yield.
