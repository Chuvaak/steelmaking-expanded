Why hot blast? A raw blast furnace tops out around 1400 °C — not enough to melt iron. To push past that you reclaim the heat it throws away.

The gas network. Everything breathes through gas pipes (from Pipes and Power Expanded). Gas comes in three kinds: ordinary Air, hot Exhaust spilling from the furnace and converter, and forced Blast (pressurised air). A few special blocks shape the flow:
- The Cornish Air Blower — a steam-engine sub-machine — pressurises Air into Blast; this is what forces gas through the cowper stoves and into the tuyeres.
- Valves open and close a line by hand — essential for swapping the cowper stoves.
- Pressure valves open automatically above their gate pressure.
- Outlets connect the network to a structure's face (a tuyere, a stove, the converter intake).
Each network tracks its gas type, volume and temperature; an open-ended pipe leaks.

The Cowper Stove — a heat regenerator. Pipe the furnace's Exhaust into a Cowper Stove and it soaks that heat into its brick core (\"charging\"); burning a coal pile inside charges it faster, with anthracite fastest. Beware: burning low grades of coal makes the stove itself produce exhaust from its main output!
Once charged, route plain Blast through the same stove and it pours out scorching hot. Feed that into the furnace tuyeres and the internal temperature jumps toward ~1700 °C.

Blocks required (per stove — you want two):
- 48 x Refractory Bricks (tier 3)
- 1 x Cowper Stove Intake (the control block)
- 4 x Heat Sink
- 2 x Gas Outlet
- 1 x Gas Passthrough
- 1 x Iron Hatch Door
One interior slot is left open for the coal pile that charges the core.

A single stove only does one job at a time, and a charged core runs down as you draw blast from it. Run two stoves as a pair: while one charges on exhaust, the other discharges hot blast into the furnace; when the discharging one cools, swap their roles with valves. Keep exhaust and air on separate paths — if they mix inside a stove it cannot heat.

The Smoke Stack. A blast furnace produces more exhaust than the stoves can swallow. The Smoke Stack vents the surplus to the sky — a backed-up exhaust line chokes and extinguishes the furnace, so treat it as the safety valve of the whole system.
Blocks required:
- 1 x Smoke Stack Intake (the control block)
- 60 x Fire Clay Bricks
